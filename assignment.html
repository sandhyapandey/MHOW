HISTORY

Mhow is a cantonment town founded in 1818 following the treaty between english and the maratha maharajas of Indore. Mhow is located 23 kilometres south of Indore city towards Mumbai on the Mumbai-Agra Road.Janapav kuti near mhow is the birth place of parsuram according to the hindu religious texts. The climate of mhow is like extremely hot and is cold in summer and winter respectively. However the other part of the year, it is moderate.

As early as 1818, the Indian Army has had a presence in Mhow. Up until World War II, Mhow was the headquarters of the 5th Division of the Southern Army. According to local legend, Winston Churchill also spent a few months in Mhow when he was a subaltern serving with his regiment in India. Mhow houses three premier training institutions of the Indian Army; The Infantry School, The Military College of Telecommunication Engineering (MCTE) The Army War College.In addition to these institutes, ARTRAC was first established in mhow. ARTRAC was based in Mhow from 1991 to 1994, before it shifted to Shimla.
